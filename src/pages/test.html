Project leads

Åsa Mäkitalo

University of Gothenburg 

asa.makitalo@ped.gu.se 

I’m Åsa Mäkitalo. My family is from the northern parts of Sweden, just on the border to Finland, which explains my name (that may seem a bit exotic). My interests are many, but professionally it is primarily geared towards communication, organization and learning. I am especially interested in the ways institutional actors communicate and intervene in our lives, shape our identities and forms of participation in society. For this reason, the SOS project that focuses on the very encounter between public institutions and the people they are to serve is so interesting to me. I have a professorship at the Department of Education, Communication and Learning at the University of Gothenburg, and in that capacity, I’m the Swedish Co-PI in this project. I have many years of experience leading interdisciplinary research environments and love learning from others and getting people from different backgrounds to work together. 

Brit Ross Winthereik 

IT University of Copenhagen 

brwi@itu.dk 

I am Head of Center for Digital Welfare at the IT University of Copenhagen – one of the consortium partners in this project – and also a full Professor of Ethnography and Science and Technology Studies (STS). In my research, I focus on infrastructures as technical, social, and political accomplishments as well as on their, sometimes, problematic effects, on equality for example. In the past, I have published extensively on public sector digitalization, information infrastructures, and ethnography for anthropology and STS. I am passionate about developing analytics and methods that allow us to grasp the complexities of the digital age, and I am a frequent participant in public debates on issues related to public sector digitalization. In this project, my role is to oversee the management of the project, supervise researchers and bring its results to various audiences. 

Margunn Aanestad 

Adger University  

margunn.aanestad@uia.no 

I am a full Professor in Information Systems at the University of Agder in Norway. I originally trained as an engineer based on a desire to understand how the technology worked and with a sense of how important technology is in shaping the society we live in. I have worked with Information and Communication Technologies (ICT) in the healthcare context and now I am the Academic Head of the Centre of e-Health, a Research Priority Area at the University of Agder. In the SOS project, I will be the main supervisor of Katherine Stacey in her journey towards a PhD and will take part in studies of how digital healthcare services can be improved in terms of accessibility and usability. 

Laura Juncker 

IT-Univerity of Copenhagen 

Laju@itu.dk 

I am a Strategic Project Manager at the Center for Digital Welfare at the IT University of Copenhagen and am thus project managing the SOS project together with Brit. I have a background in Development and Conflict Studies and have over the years become increasingly interested in the effects of technology on society and differing life experiences. I especially am especially fascinated by studying how technology perpetuates or changes existing inequalities and produces new ones, and find that projects like SOS are pivotal for inspiring sound public sector decisions relating to digitalization now and in the future.  

Michala Høg Daimar 

The Danish Agency for Digitisation 

mihda@digst.dk 

I am a secretariat leader at the digital inclusion department of the Agency for Digitisation. I worked on digital inclusion since I started working at the Danish Agency for Digitalisation six years ago. Over the years, digital inclusion has risen to the top of the political agenda and it is of course exciting to follow that journey. Comparative analysis leads

Thorben Simonsen 

IT University of Copenhagen 

thsi@itu.dk 

I am an Assistant Professor at the Department of Business IT at the IT University of Copenhagen. In my PhD, I studied the spatial organization of psychiatric practice. Now, I study the organization of, and constitutive relations between, digital technologies, spaces, and different professional practices, with a particular interest in mental healthcare and architecture. In the SOS project, I am a co-supervisor of PhD Fellow Barbara Nino Carreras, and I manage the project’s comparative analysis across Denmark, Sweden and Norway. This work entails analyzing organizational and technological conditions for informal work and interfaces with public authorities in the three countries.

Katherine Stacey, PhD Fellow

University of Adger 

 email

 
I am originally from the United States, I hold a BA in Cultural Anthropology and an MA in Applied Cultural Analysis. I am a PhD Fellow in Information Systems at the University of Agder with the Center for e-Health and the Centre for Digital Transformation. My research interests are related to design and inclusion. For the SOS project, my work focuses on finding common barriers to digital inclusion in the Norwegian healthcare system, on understanding how those challenges are overcome today, and on understanding how they might be addressed in the future. I am interested in how the notion of digital citizenship applies to individuals who are not fully integrated into the digital state and/or the Norwegian welfare state.   

Barbara Nino Carreras, PhD Fellow 

IT University of Copenhagen 

Barb@itu.dk 

I am a researcher part of the Center for Digital Welfare and the Technologies in Practice Research Group at the IT University of Copenhagen. I have a background in digital design and project management. I hold a master’s degree in Digital Innovation and Management. My PhD project investigates informal support to citizens who struggle to access and use public digital self-services in Denmark. This kind of help is what in our project we call informal welfare work. I am interested in disability studies, participation, inclusion in practice, design, accessibility, web accessibility, science and technology studies, and ethnographic methods. I am half Colombian and half Spanish. I have been living in Denmark since 2012.
